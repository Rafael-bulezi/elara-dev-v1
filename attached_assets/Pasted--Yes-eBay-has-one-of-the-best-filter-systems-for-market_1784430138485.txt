### Recommended Filter System for Elara

#### 1. Desktop Version (eBay-style)

**Layout:**
- Fixed left sidebar (around 260–280px wide)
- Main product grid on the right
- Applied filters appear as **removable chips** above the product grid

**Filter Order (most important first for Angola):**

1. **Entrega** (highest priority)
   - Rápida em Luanda (hoje/amanhã)
   - Províncias (1–5 dias)
   - Recolha na loja

2. **Preço (Kz)**
   - Min / Max inputs
   - Optional quick ranges (Até 50.000, 50.000–150.000, etc.)

3. **Marca**
   - Search box inside the filter (“Pesquisar marca”)
   - Checkboxes + counts (Samsung (128), Apple (92)...)
   - “Ver mais” if the list is long

4. **Avaliação do cliente**
   - 4★ & up, 3★ & up, etc. (with counts)

5. **Vendedor**
   - Vendedores Angolanos Verificados
   - (Later: Top Rated, etc.)

6. Other filters (Categoria, etc.) lower down

**Key Behaviors:**
- Most important filters (Entrega + Preço + Marca) should be **open by default**
- When a filter is selected → results update (or show a clear “Aplicar” if you prefer)
- Show number of results next to each option
- “Limpar tudo” at the top of the sidebar

---

#### 2. Mobile Version (Best Practice)

- Floating or top **“Filtros”** button (with a badge showing how many filters are active)
- Opens a **bottom sheet** (or full-screen panel)
- Sticky button at the bottom: **“Ver resultados (842)”**
- Applied filters shown as chips at the top of the sheet
- Same filter order as desktop
- Easy “Limpar tudo”

This is the modern standard and works better than trying to force a sidebar on mobile.

### Extra Details That Make It Feel Like eBay

- When the user selects filters, show the active ones as **chips** above the products:
  ```
  [Rápida em Luanda ×]  [Samsung ×]  [4★ & up ×]   Limpar tudo
  ```
- Clicking the × removes only that filter
- Brand list should be searchable
- Keep the design clean — don’t overcrowd the sidebar
